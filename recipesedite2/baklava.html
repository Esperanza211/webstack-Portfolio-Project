Moroccan Baklava

Ingredients

  • For the Syrup: 1 cup water 1 cup granulated sugar 1/2 cup honey 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1 teaspoon lemon juice For the Nut Filling: 1 cup walnuts, finely chopped 1 cup pistachios, finely chopped 1 cup almonds, finely chopped 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves For the Baklava: 1 package phyllo dough (16 oz), thawed 1 cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

Prepare the Syrup: In a medium saucepan, combine the water, sugar, honey, vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, and lemon juice. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes. Remove from heat and let the syrup cool completely. Prepare the Nut Filling: In a large bowl, mix the chopped walnuts, pistachios, almonds, granulated sugar, ground cinnamon, and ground cloves until well combined. Assemble the Baklava: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Brush a 9x13-inch baking dish with some of the melted butter. Unroll the phyllo dough and cut it to fit the size of your baking dish if necessary. Cover the phyllo dough with a damp cloth to prevent it from drying out. Place one sheet of phyllo dough in the bottom of the baking dish and brush it with melted butter. Repeat this process until you have layered about 8 sheets of phyllo. Sprinkle a thin layer of the nut filling over the phyllo. Add another 6-8 sheets of phyllo, brushing each with butter. Repeat the layering process with the nuts and phyllo, ending with a top layer of about 8-10 sheets of phyllo, each brushed with butter. Cut and Bake: Using a sharp knife, cut the assembled baklava into diamond or square shapes. Bake in the preheated oven for about 50-60 minutes, until the baklava is golden brown and crispy. Add the Syrup: Remove the baklava from the oven and immediately pour the cooled syrup evenly over the hot baklava. Let the baklava cool completely to absorb the syrup. Serve: Once cooled, remove the baklava pieces from the dish and serve. Store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature. Enjoy your delicious homemade baklava!
