chicken bastila

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into small pieces 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1 teaspoon ground ginger 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional) 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 tablespoons olive oil 2 eggs 1 cup chopped almonds 1 package of phyllo dough (usually found in the freezer section of most supermarkets) Powdered sugar for dusting Orange flower water for serving (optional) Step-by-Step Instructions

Instructions

In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute. Add the spices and chicken: Add the ground cinnamon, ground ginger, ground turmeric, red pepper flakes (if using), salt, and black pepper to the skillet. Cook for 1-2 minutes, until the spices are fragrant. Add the chicken pieces and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Prepare the egg mixture: In a separate bowl, beat the eggs until well combined. Add the chopped almonds and mix well. Assemble the bastilla: Thaw the phyllo dough according to the package instructions. Cut the dough into four equal pieces. Place one piece on a flat surface and brush with butter. Place a spoonful of the chicken filling in the center of the dough, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges. Brush the edges with butter. Place a spoonful of the egg mixture on top of the filling. Fold the dough over the filling, brushing the edges with butter as you go. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling. Bake the bastilla: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the bastilla on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the tops with butter and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the phyllo is golden brown. Dust with powdered sugar and serve: Remove the bastilla from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Dust with powdered sugar and serve warm. You can also drizzle with orange flower water for added flavor and aroma. Tips and Variations. To make the bastilla ahead of time, prepare the filling and assemble the bastilla, then refrigerate or freeze until baking time. You can also add other ingredients to the filling, such as chopped bell peppers or chopped fresh parsley. For a more authentic Moroccan flavor, use ras el hanout spice blend instead of individual spices. Experiment with different types of nuts, such as pistachios or hazelnuts, for added texture and flavor. Enjoy your delicious Moroccan Bastilla Djaj!
