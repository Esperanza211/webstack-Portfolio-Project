Moroccan couscous

Ingredients

  • Basic Ingredients: 2 cups couscous (traditional or instant) 2 cups water (or chicken/vegetable broth for added flavor) 1-2 tablespoons olive oil or butter 1/2 teaspoon salt Optional Add-ins: 1/2 cup chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley, cilantro, mint, or a mix) 1/4 cup toasted nuts (such as almonds, pine nuts, or pistachios) 1/4 cup dried fruits (such as raisins, apricots, or cranberries) 1 teaspoon ground cumin or other spices (such as cinnamon, turmeric, or paprika) Fresh lemon juice and zest from 1 lemon 1-2 cloves garlic, minced 1 small onion, finely chopped 1/4 cup finely chopped vegetables (such as bell peppers, carrots, or tomatoes) A pinch of saffron threads (soaked in a little warm water)

Instructions

Prepare the Liquid: In a medium saucepan, bring the water or broth to a boil. Add the salt and olive oil or butter to the boiling liquid. Add the Couscous: Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the couscous. Cover the saucepan and let it sit for 5 minutes, allowing the couscous to absorb the liquid. Fluff the Couscous: After 5 minutes, remove the lid and fluff the couscous with a fork to separate the grains. Optional Add-ins: If using, saut√© the garlic and onion in a bit of olive oil until softened and add to the couscous. Stir in the chopped fresh herbs, toasted nuts, dried fruits, spices, lemon juice, and zest. Add any finely chopped vegetables and soaked saffron if desired. Serve: Serve the couscous warm as a side dish or as a base for tagines, stews, or roasted meats and vegetables.
