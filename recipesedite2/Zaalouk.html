zaalouk

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggplants, peeled and diced 3-4 ripe tomatoes, peeled and chopped 3-4 cloves garlic, minced 1/4 cup olive oil 1 tablespoon tomato paste (optional) 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper (optional, for heat) 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste) 1/2 teaspoon black pepper (or to taste) 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped Juice of 1 lemon

Instructions

Prepare the Eggplants: Peel and dice the eggplants. Sprinkle with salt and let them sit in a colander for about 30 minutes to draw out any bitterness. Rinse and pat dry. Cook the Eggplants: In a large pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced eggplants and cook until they start to soften and brown, about 10 minutes. Add Tomatoes and Spices: Add the chopped tomatoes, minced garlic, tomato paste (if using), ground cumin, paprika, ground cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper to the pan. Stir well to combine. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low and cover the pan. Let the mixture simmer for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the eggplants are very soft and the tomatoes have broken down. Mash and Mix: Use a fork or potato masher to mash the mixture to your desired consistency. Stir in the chopped cilantro, parsley, and lemon juice. Adjust Seasoning: Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. Serve: Serve zaalouk warm or at room temperature as a side dish, dip, or spread. Drizzle with a bit of extra olive oil before serving if desired.
