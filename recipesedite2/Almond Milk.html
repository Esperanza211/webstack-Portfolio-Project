Almond Milk

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw almonds 4 cups water (for blending) 2-3 cups water (for soaking) Optional Ingredients for Flavor: 1-2 tablespoons sweetener (such as honey, maple syrup, or agave nectar) 1 teaspoon vanilla extract A pinch of salt 1-2 dates (for natural sweetness) 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional) 1 tablespoon cocoa powder (for chocolate almond milk)

Instructions

Soak the Almonds: Place the almonds in a bowl and cover them with 2-3 cups of water. Let them soak overnight, or for at least 8-12 hours. The longer they soak, the creamier the almond milk will be. After soaking, drain and rinse the almonds thoroughly. Blend the Almonds: Place the soaked almonds into a blender. Add 4 cups of fresh water to the blender. Blend on high speed for 2-3 minutes, until the almonds are finely ground and the mixture looks milky. Strain the Almond Milk: Place a nut milk bag, cheesecloth, or a fine-mesh strainer over a large bowl or jug. Pour the almond mixture through the strainer to separate the milk from the almond pulp. Use your hands to squeeze out as much milk as possible from the almond pulp. Add Flavor (Optional): If desired, return the strained almond milk to the blender. Add your choice of sweeteners, vanilla extract, salt, dates, or other flavorings. Blend again until smooth. Store the Almond Milk: Pour the almond milk into a sealed container and refrigerate. Homemade almond milk will keep for 3-4 days in the refrigerator. Shake well before each use, as it may separate. Enjoy your fresh, homemade almond milk!
