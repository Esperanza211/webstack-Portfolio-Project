mhencha sweet

Ingredients

  • For the Almond Paste Filling: 500g blanched almonds 250g granulated sugar 2 tablespoons orange blossom water 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon almond extract 100g unsalted butter, melted For the Dough: 500g phyllo pastry sheets 200g unsalted butter, melted (for brushing the pastry) For Garnish: Powdered sugar (optional) Ground cinnamon (optional) Honey (optional)

Instructions

Prepare the Almond Paste Filling: Grind the blanched almonds and sugar together in a food processor until they form a fine, crumbly mixture. Add the orange blossom water, ground cinnamon, and almond extract to the almond-sugar mixture. Mix until well combined. Gradually add the melted butter while mixing until the almond paste holds together. If it’s too dry, you can add a little more orange blossom water. Assemble the M'hanncha: Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F) and line a baking tray with parchment paper. On a clean surface, lay out one sheet of phyllo pastry and brush it with melted butter. Place another sheet on top and brush it with butter as well. Form a long, thin log with a portion of the almond paste and place it along one edge of the phyllo pastry. Roll the pastry around the almond paste to form a long tube. Repeat this process with the remaining phyllo sheets and almond paste to create multiple tubes. Starting from the center of the baking tray, coil the filled phyllo tubes around each other to form a large spiral (like a snake). Bake the M'hanncha: Brush the entire coiled pastry with melted butter. Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes or until golden brown and crispy. Garnish and Serve: Once baked, let the M'hanncha cool slightly. Optionally, drizzle with honey and sprinkle with powdered sugar and ground cinnamon before serving.
