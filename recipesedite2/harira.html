Harira soup

Ingredients

  • 500g lamb, beef, or chicken (cut into small pieces) 1 large onion, finely chopped 1 cup lentils, rinsed 1 cup chickpeas, soaked overnight and drained (or canned chickpeas) 1 cup fresh cilantro, finely chopped 1 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped 1 celery stalk, finely chopped 2 large tomatoes, peeled and pureed 1/4 cup tomato paste 1/2 cup vermicelli or broken angel hair pasta 1/2 cup rice (optional) 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 tablespoon butter (optional) 1.5 liters of water or beef/chicken broth Salt and pepper to taste Spices: 1 teaspoon ground turmeric 1 teaspoon ground ginger 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads (optional) 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper Thickener: 1/2 cup flour 1 cup cold water Garnish: Fresh lemon wedges Fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions

Prepare the Base: In a large pot, heat the olive oil and butter over medium heat. Add the meat, onion, celery, cilantro, and parsley. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the meat is browned and the onions are soft. Add the Spices: Stir in the turmeric, ginger, cinnamon, cumin, paprika, saffron, coriander, and black pepper. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are fragrant. Add the Tomatoes and Tomato Paste: Add the pureed tomatoes and tomato paste to the pot. Stir well to combine. Add Lentils and Chickpeas: Add the lentils and chickpeas to the pot. Stir well. Simmer the Soup: Pour in the water or broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat and chickpeas are tender. Add Pasta and Rice: If using, add the vermicelli and rice to the soup. Continue to simmer until the pasta and rice are cooked, about 10-15 minutes. Prepare the Thickener: In a small bowl, mix the flour with the cold water until smooth and free of lumps. Thicken the Soup: Gradually add the flour mixture to the soup, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Continue to simmer the soup for another 10-15 minutes, or until it thickens to your desired consistency. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve with lemon wedges on the side for squeezing over the soup. Enjoy your delicious and hearty Harira soup!
