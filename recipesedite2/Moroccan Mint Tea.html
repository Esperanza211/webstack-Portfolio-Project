Moroccan tea

Ingredients

  • Fresh mint leaves: A handful (about 15-20 leaves) Green tea: 1-2 teaspoons (Chinese gunpowder green tea is commonly used) Sugar: 2-3 tablespoons (adjust to taste) Boiling water: 4 cups

Instructions

Boil Water: Bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Rinse the Teapot: Rinse a teapot with a little boiling water to warm it up. Add Green Tea: Add 1-2 teaspoons of green tea to the teapot. First Rinse of Tea: Pour a small amount of boiling water into the teapot, swirl it around, and then discard this water. This step helps to rinse and open up the tea leaves. Brew the Tea: Add a handful of fresh mint leaves and 2-3 tablespoons of sugar to the teapot. Fill the teapot with the remaining boiling water. Let the tea steep for about 3-5 minutes. Mix the Tea: To mix the ingredients well, pour the tea into a glass and then return it to the teapot. Repeat this process 2-3 times. Serve: Serve the tea hot, traditionally pouring it from a height to create a frothy top. Enjoy your Moroccan mint tea!
