chebakia

Ingredients

  • 3 1/2 cups white flour (all-purpose or bread) 1/4 tsp dry yeast (less in very warm weather) 1/2 cup fine semolina (can use coarser caliber if you like) 1/4 cup very soft unsalted butter (more if needed). Vegetable oil, for frying 2 cups honey 1/4 cup orange blossom water 1/4 cup toasted sesame seeds, for garnish

Instructions

Prepare the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, ground sesame seeds, ground almonds, ground anise seeds, ground cinnamon, ground mastic gum, ground cloves, ground nutmeg, and ground saffron. Make a well in the center and add the melted butter, vegetable oil, orange blossom water, white vinegar, egg, and salt. Gradually add the warm water while mixing to form a soft dough. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for 15 minutes. Shape the Chebakia: Divide the dough into small portions and roll each portion into a thin sheet. Cut the dough into small rectangles (about 2x4 inches) and make four parallel cuts inside each rectangle, leaving the edges uncut. Take each rectangle, weave and pinch the strips together to form a flower shape. Fry the Chebakia: Heat the vegetable oil in a deep fryer or a large pot over medium heat. Fry the shaped dough in batches until golden brown and crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Coat with Honey: In a separate pot, warm the honey and orange blossom water over low heat. Dip the fried chebakia into the warm honey mixture, ensuring they are well-coated. Remove from the honey and place on a wire rack to drain. Garnish: Sprinkle the chebakia with toasted sesame seeds while they are still sticky from the honey. Serve: Allow the chebakia to cool completely before serving. Store in an airtight container to keep them fresh. Enjoy your homemade Chebakia!
